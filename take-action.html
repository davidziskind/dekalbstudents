Take Action

Board members respond to constituent pressure. Here are the most effective things you can do right now — ranked by impact.

Highest Impact

Sign the Petition

A petition with thousands of signatures is a concrete, countable signal to board members. Sign, and share with every parent you know — in your school and across DeKalb.

Sample Email Template

Dear [Board Member Name],

My name is [Your Name], and I am the parent of [number] children who attend [School Name] in DeKalb County. I am writing to urge you to vote against the proposed closure of [School Name] in the Student Assignment Project (SAP).

[School Name] is more than a building — it is the foundation of our neighborhood. [Add 1–2 sentences specific to your school and your family's experience.]

I am deeply concerned that the SAP process has not adequately accounted for [choose: the community's input / the flawed utilization data / the true costs of displacement / the impact on vulnerable students]. I urge you to pause the closure timeline and pursue alternatives before making an irreversible decision.

I would welcome the opportunity to speak with you directly. I will also be present at the public comment session on [UPDATE DATE].

Respectfully,
[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[Your Email / Phone]

3
High Impact

Attend a Meeting & Speak

Showing up in person — and speaking during public comment — is one of the most powerful things you can do. Board members notice packed rooms. A three-minute statement that shares a personal story is more memorable than a form letter.

Tips for Public Comment

  • You'll typically have 2–3 minutes. Practice beforehand.
  • State your name, address, and which school(s) you represent.
  • Lead with a specific, personal story — not statistics.
  • End with a clear ask: "I am asking you to vote NO on the closure of [School Name]."
  • Bring a friend — a packed room sends a message before anyone speaks.
4
Always Helpful

Spread the Word

Share this site with every DeKalb parent you know. The more people who are informed and engaged, the harder it is for the board to ignore community opposition.
